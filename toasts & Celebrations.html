[Immortal Sails]

                Now, in a breath, we’ll burst those gates of gold,
                And ransack heaven before our moment fails.
                

                Now, in a breath, before we, too, grow old,
                We’ll mount and sing and spread immortal sails.
                

                It is not time that makes eternity.
                Love and an hour may quite out-span the years,
                

                And give us more to hear and more to see
                Than life can wash away with all its tears.
                

                Dear, when we part, at last, that sunset sky
                Shall not be touched with deeper hues than this;
                

                But we shall ride the lightning ere we die
                And seize our brief infinitude of bliss,
                

                With time to spare for all that heaven can tell,
                While eyes meet eyes, and look their last farewell.

About the Poet

1880–1958

Alfred Noyes was a prolific and popular writer who made a living from writing

when few others could. He was particularly influenced by Tennyson,

His unique style featured spare and precise poems that experimented

Wordsworth, Chaucer, and Shakespeare, and his work often had a Romantic

and ballad-like style, with optimistic themes inspired by nature. Noyes

valued traditional English verse and disliked modernist movements, particularly

the work of James Joyce, though some critics criticized him for

being resistant to change and literary evolution.
