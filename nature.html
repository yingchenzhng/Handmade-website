Nature

[I was washing at night out in the yard]

                    I was washing at night out in the yard—
                    the heavens glowing with rough stars.
                    
A star-beam like salt upon an axe,

                    the water barrel brimful and cold.
                    

                    A padlock makes the gate secure,
                    and conscience gives sternness to the earth—
                    
hard to find a standard anywhere

                    purer than the truth of new-made cloth.
                    

                    A star melts in the barrel like salt,
                    and the ice-cold winter is blacker still,
                    
death is more pure, disaster saltier

                    and earth more truthful and more terrible.

About the Poet

1891–1938

Osip Mandelstam was a significant Russian poet born in Poland

in the late 1800s, who lived in St. Petersburg with his family.

He studied abroad in France and Germany and gained acceptance into an exclusive

Christian institution in St. Petersburg, despite being of Jewish ancestry.

Mandelstam published his first verse collection, "Stone," which established

him as one of Russia's upper echelon poets. He identified as an Acmeist,

which meant his poetry was rooted in intuition and humanism. However,

during the rise of the Bolsheviks, Mandelstam refused to compromise his art for political aims,

political aims, leading to his alienation from other pro-state artists and intellectuals
